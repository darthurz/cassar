Membership

Join the Sons of the American Revolution

If one of your ancestors contributed to American independence, you may be eligible. Getting started is straightforward — and we have people ready to help.

Home› Join
How It Works

Four Steps to Membership

01

Determine Eligibility

Verify that you have a lineal male ancestor who contributed to the American Revolution — as a soldier, seaman, militiaman, signer of the Declaration, or recognized patriot.

02

Contact a Local Chapter

Reach out to the VP of Membership, Scott Whitman, or contact a registrar at your nearest California SAR chapter directly. We have 24 chapters across the state.

03

Collect Your Documentation

Gather genealogical records tracing your lineage to your patriot ancestor. Your chapter registrar will explain exactly what is needed and help you obtain records.

04

Submit Your Application

Complete your application through the SAR Online Application Form System. Your chapter registrar reviews and submits it for national approval.

Who Is Eligible to Join?

  • Male, age 18 or older
  • A citizen of good repute in the community
  • A lineal descendant of an American Revolution patriot
  • Does not advocate overthrow of the U.S. Government
  • Ancestors may include soldiers, sailors, militia, minutemen
  • Signers of the Declaration of Independence
  • Members of Committees of Safety or Correspondence
  • Any recognized patriot who performed overt acts of resistance to British authority
Junior Members

Young Men Under 18

Young men under the age of 18 who meet all other requirements may apply as Junior Members. Junior membership is a great way to begin building your connection to American history and to establish your genealogical record early. Contact your local chapter for details on junior membership programs.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my ancestor was a patriot?

Many ancestors served without leaving obvious records. The SAR's Patriot Research System contains over 200,000 verified patriot ancestors. Your chapter registrar and our genealogist can help you search records to confirm your lineage. Contact us and we will help you investigate.

How long does the application process take?

The process typically takes 3–6 months, depending on how readily available your genealogical records are and how quickly documentation can be gathered. Your chapter registrar will guide you at every step.

What if I do not know much about my family history?

That is perfectly fine — and quite common. The SAR has extensive genealogical resources including the Patriot Research System, record copy services, and a network of genealogical assistants who can help you trace your lineage. Getting started is easier than most people expect.

Is there an annual membership fee?

Yes, there are national, state, and chapter dues. Your chapter registrar will provide current fee information. The California SAR is a 501(c)(3) non-profit, and dues help fund our educational, patriotic, and community programs.

Can I be a member if I live outside California?

Your chapter affiliation is typically based on your residence. If you live in California, you join a California chapter. If you are relocating or have questions about which state society is right for you, the National SAR can help direct you.

What are the benefits of membership?

Members join a community of men with deep shared heritage, gain access to genealogical resources, participate in patriotic programs and events, connect with local chapters, support youth education, and establish a permanent link to the founding generation of the United States.

Ready to Get Started?

Contact the California SAR Vice President of Membership — he will connect you with your nearest chapter registrar and answer any questions.
