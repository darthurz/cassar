Youth Programs

Inspiring the Next Generation of Patriots

California SAR youth programs connect students, teachers, and young people across the state with the history, ideals, and stories of the American Revolution.

Elementary School

SAR Americanism Poster Contest

The annual Americanism Poster Contest invites elementary school students to create original artwork celebrating American history and patriotism. The contest inspires creativity while connecting young students to the story of the American Revolution.

  • Open to 5th grade students
  • Theme changes annually, focusing on American history
  • Winners compete at chapter, state, and national levels
  • Cash prizes awarded at each level

Middle School

SAR Middle School Brochure Contest

Middle school students create informational brochures on topics related to the American Revolution. The contest develops research, writing, and design skills while deepening students' understanding of the founding era.

  • Open to students in grades 6–8
  • Brochures must be original research and writing
  • Winners advance to state and national competitions
  • Cash prizes and certificates awarded

All Ages

SAR Youth Video Contest

The Youth Video Contest challenges students to produce short documentary-style videos on topics related to the American Revolution. The program develops digital media skills and encourages deep engagement with primary sources and historical research.

  • Open to students in various grade levels
  • Videos must address a Revolutionary War topic
  • Strong emphasis on original research and storytelling
  • Winners recognized at state and national levels

Scouting

Eagle Scout Awards

The California SAR honors Eagle Scouts through two distinct programs recognizing the highest achievement in Boy Scouting, connecting the values of scouting to the patriotic ideals of the American Revolution.

SAR Eagle Scout Certificate: Presented to every Eagle Scout upon earning their rank, signed by the California SAR President.

Arthur M. & Berdena King Eagle Scout Award: A competitive national award recognizing exceptional Eagle Scouts who demonstrate outstanding citizenship, patriotism, and community service.

Robert E. Burt Boy Scout Volunteer Award: Recognizes adult Scouting volunteers who make extraordinary contributions to youth through the Scouting program.

High School

George S. & Stella M. Knight Essay Contest

High school students write original essays on topics related to the American Revolution, the founding era, and the principles established by the patriots of 1776. The contest develops research and writing skills while deepening historical understanding.

  • Open to students in grades 9–12
  • Essays must be original, researched work
  • Winners advance to state and national competitions
  • Significant cash awards at each level

High School

SAR Oration Contest

High school students deliver original orations on topics related to the American Revolution and its lasting significance. The Joseph S. Rumbaugh Historical Oration Contest develops public speaking skills and patriotic knowledge.

  • Open to students in grades 9–12
  • Orations must be original compositions
  • Contestants judged on content, delivery, and patriotic themes
  • Winners compete at chapter, state, and national levels

High School / College

ROTC / JROTC Recognition Program

The California SAR recognizes outstanding ROTC and JROTC cadets who demonstrate exceptional leadership, patriotism, and academic achievement. The program honors the connection between today's military programs and the patriots of the Revolution.

  • Open to ROTC and JROTC cadets at high schools and colleges
  • Recognition presented at unit ceremonies
  • Certificate and medal from the California SAR
  • Nominated by commanding officers

NSCC / NLCC Cadet Program

The California SAR recognizes outstanding Naval Sea Cadet Corps (NSCC) and Naval League Cadet Corps (NLCC) cadets for their dedication, leadership, and patriotic service.

Educators

Teacher Recognition Programs

The California SAR recognizes outstanding history educators through two major programs celebrating teachers who inspire a love of American history in their students.

Valley Forge Teacher of the Year: Recognizes a K–12 teacher in California who has demonstrated excellence in teaching American history, particularly relating to the Revolutionary period.

Dr. Tom & Betty Lawrence American History Teacher Award: Grants of up to $1,000 to support history teachers in developing innovative curriculum and programs related to the American Revolution.

Spirit of America Middle School Program

The Spirit of America program recognizes outstanding middle school students who demonstrate exceptional citizenship, patriotism, and commitment to American ideals. Chapter members visit middle schools throughout California to present this award.

  • Nomination by school faculty and administrators
  • Recognition at school ceremonies
