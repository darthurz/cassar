Our Story

Who We Are

The Sons of the American Revolution honors our Revolutionary War patriot ancestors by promoting patriotism, serving our communities, and educating and inspiring future generations about the founding principles of our nation.

California's connection to the SAR runs deep. The predecessor organization — the Sons of Revolutionary Sires — was founded on July 4, 1876, right here in San Francisco, California. Acting on a resolution by the New Jersey Society, delegates from thirteen state societies met at Fraunces Tavern in New York City on April 30, 1889, celebrating the centennial of George Washington's inauguration. On that date, the National Society of the Sons of the American Revolution was organized.

The National Society was incorporated in Connecticut on January 17, 1890, and later chartered by the United States Congress on June 6, 1906. Today, the California Society maintains 24 active chapters throughout the state.

The California SAR is a 501(c)(3) non-profit corporation. We are non-partisan and welcome all men of good repute who can establish descent from a patriot of the American Revolution.

Our Mission

To honor Revolutionary War patriot ancestors by promoting patriotism, serving communities, and educating future generations about America's founding principles.

Our Purpose

Organized to "perpetuate the memory of those who, by their services or sacrifices during the war of the American Revolution, achieved the independence of the American People."

What We Do

We decorate the graves of Revolutionary War soldiers, support excellence in history education, participate in patriotic observances, and assist with genealogical research and preservation of historic material.

Who We Are

The SAR is the largest male genealogical society in the country, with tens of thousands of active members in over 550 chapters across the United States and internationally.

Our History

Timeline

July 4, 1876

Sons of Revolutionary Sires Founded

The predecessor organization is founded in San Francisco, California — planting the seeds of the SAR on Independence Day.

April 30, 1889

National Society Organized

Delegates from 13 state societies meet at Fraunces Tavern, New York City, on the 100th anniversary of Washington's inauguration, formally organizing the National Society.

January 17, 1890

Incorporated in Connecticut

The National Society of the Sons of the American Revolution is formally incorporated.

June 6, 1906

Chartered by U.S. Congress

The SAR receives an official charter from the United States Congress, cementing its status as a national patriotic organization.

Today

24 California Chapters Active

The California Society thrives with 24 chapters from San Diego to the Redwood Empire, continuing the work of honoring patriot ancestors and serving communities.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

American Freedom

We maintain and extend the institutions of American freedom, ensuring the principles of the Revolution remain alive for every generation.

True Patriotism

We cultivate an appreciation for authentic patriotism — love of country expressed through service, sacrifice, and civic engagement.

National Symbols

We foster deep respect for the American flag, the Constitution, and the national symbols that unite us as a people.

Citizenship

We uphold the value of American citizenship — the rights and responsibilities that flow from the sacrifices of our patriot ancestors.

E Pluribus Unum

We celebrate the unifying force of "out of many, one" — the ideal that from people of many nations, one nation and one people emerged.

Education

We invest in educating youth and communities about the history, ideals, and individuals who shaped the American Revolution and our republic.
