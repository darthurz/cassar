What California SAR Members Do

From honoring veterans to marking historic sites, California SAR members actively serve their communities and preserve the legacy of the American Revolution across the Golden State.

The 250th Anniversary of American Independence

On July 4, 2026, America will celebrate 250 years of independence. The California SAR is working to become a major recognized voice of the American Revolution leading up to this historic milestone. Find resources, events, and ways to participate.

Color Guard

The California SAR Color Guard is a ceremonial unit of members who participate in patriotic events, parades, memorial services, and commemorative ceremonies throughout the state.

Dressed in period-appropriate attire or formal regalia, Color Guard members carry the American flag and SAR banner at public events, adding dignity and historical resonance to patriotic observances.

  • Participation in patriotic parades and ceremonies
  • Memorial Day and Veterans Day observances
  • Wreaths Across America participation
  • Chapter and state meeting ceremonies
  • Community patriotic events and school programs

Veteran Recognition

The California SAR deeply honors the men and women who have served and continue to serve in the United States Armed Forces. Our veteran recognition programs connect the legacy of the Revolution to today's defenders of freedom.

Members actively support active-duty military personnel, assist veterans in their communities, and participate in ceremonies that recognize military service and sacrifice.

  • Recognition of active-duty and veteran military members
  • Support for veteran community organizations
  • Participation in military commissioning ceremonies
  • Wreaths Across America — decorating veterans' graves
  • Veterans Day ceremony participation

Community Awards

The California SAR recognizes outstanding citizens, first responders, law enforcement officers, and community leaders who exemplify the ideals of American patriotism, service, and civic duty.

Award programs honor individuals who, in the spirit of the patriots of 1776, give selflessly to their communities and help make America stronger.

  • Law Enforcement Commendation
  • Fire Department Commendation
  • Distinguished Citizen Award
  • Public Service Award
  • Good Citizenship Award

Historical Marking

The Historical Marking program places commemorative markers and monuments at significant Revolutionary War and patriotically important sites throughout California, preserving history for future generations.

California may be far from the eastern battlefields, but it has a rich history connected to the founding era — from the missions that were active during the Revolution to the Gold Rush–era patriots who shaped the state.

  • Placement of bronze markers at historic sites
  • Dedication ceremonies for new markers
  • Research and documentation of California patriot history
  • Coordination with local historical societies
  • Maintenance of existing historical markers

Patriot Grave Marking

The Patriot Grave Marking program ensures that the graves of Revolutionary War patriots buried in California are permanently identified and honored with appropriate markers, ensuring their contributions are never forgotten.

Many patriot graves in California lack proper identification or commemoration. SAR members research, locate, and mark these graves with official patriot grave markers.

  • Research and identification of patriot graves in California
  • Placement of official SAR patriot grave markers
  • Memorial ceremonies at patriot grave sites
  • Coordination with cemetery administrators
  • Records documentation and preservation

Ladies Auxiliary

The Ladies Auxiliary of the California SAR supports and enhances the work of the Society, welcoming the spouses, mothers, daughters, and sisters of SAR members into an active and engaged community.

The Auxiliary plays a vital role in California SAR events, social programs, and community outreach, adding depth and richness to the organization's work.

  • Active participation in SAR events and programs
  • Social and educational gatherings
  • Support for SAR community and youth programs
  • Coordination with the National Ladies Auxiliary (NLASAR)
