About the California SAR

Who We Are

The Sons of the American Revolution honors our Revolutionary War patriot ancestors by promoting patriotism, serving our communities, and educating and inspiring future generations about the founding principles of our nation.

California's connection to the SAR is deep: the predecessor organization, the Sons of Revolutionary Sires, was founded on July 4, 1876 — right here in San Francisco. The National Society was formally organized on April 30, 1889, and chartered by the United States Congress in 1906.

Today, the California Society maintains 23 chapters throughout the state, bringing together men who are direct descendants of those who served in the cause of American independence.

We decorate graves of Revolutionary War soldiers, support excellence in history education, participate in patriotic observances, and preserve historical material relating to the men and women who fought for independence.

Discover Your Heritage

Our Mission

To honor Revolutionary War patriot ancestors by promoting patriotism, serving communities, and educating future generations about America's founding principles.

Our Purpose

Organized to "perpetuate the memory of those who, by their services or sacrifices during the war of the American Revolution, achieved the independence of the American People."

What We Do

We support veterans, conduct living history education, assist with genealogical research, participate in patriotic ceremonies, and recognize outstanding citizens and youth.

Our Values

We uphold the institutions of American freedom, patriotism, respect for national symbols, the value of citizenship, and the unifying force of e pluribus unum.

Membership

Join the Sons of the American Revolution

If one of your ancestors signed a founding document, fought for independence, or assisted the cause of the American Revolution — you can join the SAR. It is the largest male genealogical society in the country.

01

Determine Eligibility

Verify you have a lineal male ancestor who contributed to the American Revolution — as a soldier, seaman, signer of the Declaration, or patriot of record.

02

Contact a Local Chapter

Reach out to the Vice President of Membership or your nearest California SAR chapter registrar, who will guide you through the process.

03

Collect Your Documentation

Gather genealogical records connecting you to your patriot ancestor. The chapter registrar will help you understand what documentation is required.

04

Submit Your Application

Complete your application through the SAR Online Application Form System, reviewed and submitted with your local chapter registrar's assistance.

Who is Eligible to Join?

  • Age 18 or older (junior memberships available for those under 18)
  • A citizen of good repute in the community
  • A lineal descendant of an American Revolution patriot
  • Does not advocate overthrow of the U.S. Government
  • Patriot ancestors include: soldiers, sailors, militia members, minutemen
  • Signers of the Declaration of Independence
  • Members of Committees of Safety or Correspondence
  • Any recognized patriot with overt acts of resistance to British authority
